OBJECTIVES OF THE CONFERENCE

To critically examine the architectural and cultural heritage of South Asia through historical, material, and socio-cultural perspectives
To foster interdisciplinary and inter-institutional collaboration among academicians, researchers, and practitioners
To explore indigenous knowledge systems and traditional building practices as models for sustainability and climate resilience
To introduce and discuss digital documentation tools such as photogrammetry, LiDAR, GIS, and BIM in heritage studies
To encourage high-quality research publication and scholarly discourse in architectural heritage studies

▶ CONFERENCE THEME ◀

The conference is structured around four thematic tracks:

• Histories and Narratives in South Asian Architectural Heritage

The theme welcomes contributions on:

  1. Dynastic patronage and the politics of architectural production
  2. Shared histories, folklore, and collective memory related to built heritage
  3. The role of religious and ritual practices in shaping spatial narratives
  4. Historiographical approaches: Post-colonial interpretations
  5. Regional architectural expressions and their historical continuities
  6. Intersections of migration, trade, and architecture in shaping cross-cultural environments

• Indigenous Knowledge Systems and Building Practices

The theme welcomes contributions on:

  1. Regional typologies and construction techniques rooted in indigenous knowledge
  2. Material culture and ecological sustainability in traditional architecture
  3. Community-led building traditions and craft networks
  4. Intangible aspects of traditional practices
  5. Case studies of living heritage and intergenerational transmission
  6. Relevance to contemporary sustainable design and climate adaptation

• Digital Documentation and Technology in Heritage Studies

The theme welcomes contributions on:

  1. Case studies using digital tools
  2. Integration of GIS and spatial data
  3. Role of BIM for historic structures
  4. Photogrammetry and 3D modelling
  5. Digital archiving and accessibility
  6. Critical reflections on ethics and authenticity

• Conservation Strategies and Adaptive Reuse

The theme welcomes contributions on:

  1. Innovative approaches to architectural conservation in South Asian contexts
  2. Principles and ethics of restoration, reconstruction, and repair
  3. Adaptive reuse projects: strategies, successes, and challenges
  4. Community involvement and participatory conservation models
  5. Policy frameworks, heritage legislation, and institutional roles
  6. Case studies highlighting resilience, continuity, and transformation
